# Research Summary: Fort Property Developments

## Key Findings

### Stack

**Recommended:** Next.js 14 + Tailwind CSS + Vercel hosting + Formspree for contact forms

No backend needed for v1. Canva exports directly into `/public/` as SVG/PNG assets. Deployment is free on Vercel. This stack is maintainable, fast, and scales when Fort adds more projects.

### Features: What Top BC Developer Sites Have

**Non-negotiables:**
- Professional logo + consistent brand
- Real contact info visible (not just a form)
- Projects page — even "Coming Soon" with renders
- Founder name + photo on About (credibility anchor when there's no portfolio)
- Mobile-first responsive design
- BC-specific trust signals: warranty info, service area, affiliations (CHBA, HAVAN)

**What separates the best:**
- Neighborhood narrative ("why this location")
- Architectural-quality renders even at planning stage
- Subtle scroll animations
- VIP interest registration for projects
- Founder video (optional but high-impact)

### Architecture: Site Structure

**4 pages for launch:** Home → About → Projects → Contact

Home page hero is the #1 conversion moment — must have dark overlay on architectural image, strong headline, and prominent "Register Interest" CTA.

Projects page strategy for pre-launch: Show projects as "Planning" with renders and a Register Interest CTA. This captures leads even with no completed work.

### Watch Out For

1. **Legal:** "Register Interest" CTA only (no deposits/commitments without BCFSA Disclosure Statement)
2. **Privacy Policy** page required (PIPA BC) — use free generator, link in footer
3. **Generic copy:** Avoid all "committed to excellence" language — be specific
4. **Stock photos:** Never — use architectural renders or genuine local photography
5. **Logo scalability:** Test at 32px favicon size before finalizing
6. **No founder story = no trust** at pre-launch stage — Dennis's story is the credibility
