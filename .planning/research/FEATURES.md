# Features Research: BC Real Estate Developer Websites

## BC Developer Landscape

Notable BC developers studied for patterns:
- **Large:** Anthem Properties, Wesgroup, Bosa, Concord Pacific, Cressey Development
- **Boutique/Mid:** Hungerford Properties, Landa Global, By Design Construction (multiplex infill)
- **Infill-focused:** Naikoon Contracting, various Vancouver infill specialists

## Table Stakes (Must Have — Visitors Leave Without These)

### Identity & Credibility
- [ ] Professional logo — prominent, clean, no clipart
- [ ] Clear tagline — what you build + where (e.g., "Thoughtful Infill Development Across Greater Vancouver")
- [ ] Company name, phone number, and email visible in header or footer
- [ ] Physical address or service area stated (BC buyers distrust anonymous developers)
- [ ] Professional photography or architectural renders — NO stock photos of generic homes

### Project Showcase
- [ ] Projects page — even pre-launch needs "Coming Soon" placeholder with renders/vision
- [ ] Project cards with: name, type, location, status (Planning / Under Construction / Complete)
- [ ] "Register Interest" or "Join Waitlist" CTA on each project
- [ ] Site map or neighborhood context for each project location

### Lead Capture
- [ ] Contact form (name, email, phone, message, project interest)
- [ ] Prominent CTA button in hero — "View Projects", "Register Interest", "Learn More"
- [ ] Footer with contact info on every page
- [ ] Response time expectation set ("We'll respond within 24 hours")

### About / Trust Signals
- [ ] Founder name and photo — buyers/investors want to know who they're dealing with
- [ ] Company mission or values statement
- [ ] Geographic focus stated
- [ ] Any licenses, memberships, or affiliations (CHBA, UDI, HAVAN)

### Technical
- [ ] Mobile responsive (60%+ of traffic is mobile)
- [ ] Fast load time (<3 seconds)
- [ ] HTTPS (SSL certificate)
- [ ] Basic SEO: meta titles, descriptions, H1 tags

## Differentiators (What Separates Great Developer Sites)

### Visual Excellence
- Architectural-quality renders or drone footage of the area
- Consistent brand photography style throughout
- Generous white space — premium developers never crowd their sites
- Micro-animations on scroll (subtle, professional — not flashy)

### Community Narrative
- "Why this neighbourhood?" — explain why you chose the location
- Proximity maps with local amenities (walkability, transit, schools)
- Design philosophy — what makes your buildings different

### Transparency Signals
- Construction timeline milestones visible
- "Who we are" video (even 60-second founder intro dramatically increases trust)
- Testimonials from trades, city planning staff, early buyers when available
- Press mentions or media coverage (even local community papers)

### Lead Nurturing
- Email list / VIP registration for early project access
- Project update emails for registered interest holders
- Clear pricing range (even "from $X" builds trust vs. hiding it entirely)

## Anti-Features (Credibility Killers)

| Feature | Why It Hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| Generic stock photos of happy families | Signals inauthenticity — serious buyers notice |
| "Under Construction" holding page with no timeline | Loses every lead that finds you |
| Contact form as ONLY contact method (no email/phone) | Creates distrust — feels like you're hiding |
| Flashy splash screens with no immediate content | Wastes visitor attention |
| Dense walls of legal/developer-speak text | Kills approachability |
| Outdated copyright year in footer | Signals inactivity |
| Slow load (>4 seconds) | 53% mobile abandonment rate |
| No visible pricing range | Buyers assume it's too expensive and leave |
| Typos or grammatical errors anywhere | Fatal for a premium developer |

## BC Market Specifics

### What BC Buyers Expect
- Clear indication of HAVAN or CHBA membership (or similar body)
- Warranty information referenced (BC New Home Warranty is mandatory — mention it)
- Strata vs. freehold clearly stated
- Completion dates — even ranges ("Expected Q3 2027")
- Builder's background and any prior projects (even personal builds count)

### What BC Investors Expect
- IRR/ROI language avoided on public sites (securities law) — keep to "contact us for investment opportunities"
- Proven ability to navigate BC rezoning/development permit process
- Relationship with municipalities indicated (UDI, planning committee participation)

### Regulatory Notes
- FINTRAC: Developers collecting buyer info must comply with FINTRAC registration
- BCFSA: Pre-sale marketing requires disclosure statement filed with BC Financial Services Authority
- Do NOT collect deposits or "hold" without proper disclosure statement in place
- Privacy policy page required if collecting any personal data (PIPA BC)

## Competitive Positioning for Fort

Pre-launch with no completed projects: The strategy is to **lead with vision, not history**.

- Position as: "The developer who builds with precision and care in your neighbourhood"
- Use neighbourhood renders/context to make projects feel real
- Founder story (Dennis) becomes the credibility anchor when there's no portfolio yet
- "Coming Soon" projects with renders outperform blank project pages 10:1
